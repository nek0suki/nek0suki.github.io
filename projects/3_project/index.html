La Jolla Renderer Extensions

Disney Principled BRDFs and Volumetric Path Tracing

In this project, I implemented two extensions in an educational renderer, La Jolla Renderer.

Disney Principled BRDFs

This follows the famous SIGGRAPH course in 2012. Disney principled BRDFs. It contains diffuse, metal, glass, clearcoat, and sheen components.

An array of material balls with different UBER parameters.
A coffee maker from Mitsuba3 scenes.

Volumetric Path Tracing

This implements the Ratio Tracking method to render heterogeneous medium. It applys a majorant value of the volume and determines whether rays hitting a real or fake particle according to the ratio of the extinction coefficient at the point and the majorant (aka. homogenization).

Heterogeneous chromatic volumes.
Some other volumes.

In addition, I also implements Equiangular Sampling for circular light sources as a bonus. It gives a higher weight to places closer to light sources during importance sampling, so as to sample paths with fewer decades caused by scatter and absorption of the volume. Moreover, this is combined with the transmittance sampling method with multiple importance sampling to further increase efficiency.

Left: Transmittance sampling only. Middle: Equiangular sampling only. Right: MIS Combined. Note the differences between the images at the center and the boundary of the sphere.
